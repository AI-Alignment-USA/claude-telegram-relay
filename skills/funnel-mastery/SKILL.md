## THE SECRET FORMULA (Foundation — Do This First)

Four questions that precede everything. If you skip these, nothing else works.

1. **WHO** — Who is your dream customer? (Not "everyone." One specific person.)
2. **WHERE** — Where do they congregate online? (Facebook groups, podcasts, email lists, YouTube channels, subreddits)
3. **BAIT** — What will you offer to grab them from the congregation?
4. **RESULT** — What is the big result you're promising?

> "Step number one was figure out who is my dream customer. I changed it, got a different dream customer, it changed my life, changed my business." — Brunson

## HOOK, STORY, OFFER (The Universal Diagnostic)

**Every element of your funnel is a Hook, Story, Offer.** If something isn't converting, the problem is ALWAYS one of these three.

| Element | What It Does | Where It Appears |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **HOOK** | Grabs attention, stops the scroll | Ad headlines, email subjects, video thumbnails, first 3 seconds |
| **STORY** | Increases perceived value through emotional connection | Ad body, landing page copy, email body, video middle |
| **OFFER** | What you're trading (not just a product — a bundle of value) | CTA, checkout page, upsell page, email CTA |

**Diagnostic Process:** When a funnel underperforms:
1. Look at the ad — is the hook, story, or offer broken?
2. Look at the landing page — hook, story, or offer?
3. Go page by page through the entire funnel
4. Fix the broken element

> "If something's broken it's always either the hook, the story, or the offer." — Brunson

**An OFFER is NOT a product.** A product is a supplement bottle on Amazon. An offer is that bottle BUNDLED with a weight loss guide, calorie tracker, and meal plan. Offers outperform products every time.

## THE VALUE LADDER

A staircase of ascending offers where each step increases BOTH value delivered AND price charged.

```
                                    ┌─────────────────┐
                                    │  HIGH TICKET     │ $10K+
                                    │  (Done-for-you)  │
                                ┌───┴─────────────────┘
                                │  MID TICKET          │ $500-$5K
                                │  (Courses/Coaching)  │
                            ┌───┴──────────────────────┘
                            │  LOW TICKET               │ $10-$100
                            │  (Digital products)       │
                        ┌───┴───────────────────────────┘
                        │  FREE / TRIPWIRE               │ $0-$10
                        │  (Lead magnets, free+shipping)  │
                    ┌───┴────────────────────────────────┘
                    │  FREE CONTENT                        │
                    │  (YouTube, social, blog)              │
────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key Principles:**
- "People will continue to buy from you until you either offend them or you stop offering them things to buy"
- Each step increases the relationship AND the price
- The dating analogy: Don't propose on the first date. First date → second date → hold hands → eventually marriage.
- "The person who can spend the most money to acquire a customer always wins." — Dan Kennedy

## THREE TYPES OF OFFERS (From Dan Kennedy via Brunson)

| Type | Definition | Example | Power Level |
|------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **REPAIR** | Fixing what's broken | "We'll fix your website" | Lowest — commodity |
| **IMPROVEMENT** | Making something better (uses "ER" words: faster, better) | "Like Mailchimp only better" | Medium — competitive |
| **NEW OPPORTUNITY** | Entirely new category | iPod: "1,000 songs in your pocket" | Highest — category of one |

**Rule:** Lead your value ladder with NEW OPPORTUNITY offers at the front end. Use improvement and repair as you ascend.
- Bottom of ladder: New opportunity (books, free offers, lead magnets)
- Middle: Improvement (courses, coaching)
- Top: Repair (done-for-you, consulting)

> "A new opportunity will outconvert any improvement offer all day long." — Brunson

## THE ATTRACTIVE CHARACTER

The persona behind your brand. Four elements + four identity types.

### Four Elements:
1. **BACKSTORY** — Your origin story that your dream customer relates to
2. **PARABLES** — Stories and analogies (Jesus spoke in parables because they're memorable and relatable)
3. **FLAWS** — Show imperfections. Superman was boring until kryptonite. Be reachable, not perfect.
4. **POLARITY** — Take bold stances. Don't be wishy-washy. Attract your tribe by repelling others.

| **THE REPORTER** | Investigates, interviews, uncovers truth. | Podcasters, interviewers |
| **THE RELUCTANT HERO** | Doesn't want spotlight but shares from duty. | Introverts with valuable knowledge |

## THE EPIPHANY BRIDGE

A storytelling script that leads prospects to the same "aha moment" you had, so buying feels like THEIR idea.

### Structure:
1. **Backstory** — What gives you a vested interest
2. **Desires** — What you wanted (external AND internal)
3. **The Wall** — The obstacle you hit
4. **The Epiphany** — Your breakthrough moment
5. **The Plan** — What you did after the epiphany
6. **Conflict** — Obstacles during execution
7. **Achievement** — What you accomplished
8. **Transformation** — The before-and-after

### Rules:
- Speak at a **third-grade level** — no jargon. "Ketones are like little firefighters putting out fire in a house."
- People buy on **emotion**, justify with **logic**
- Never lead with the product — lead with the story
- Break false beliefs WITHIN the story

> "The decision to buy should feel like it was their idea all along." — Brunson

## THE BIG DOMINO STATEMENT

The ONE belief that, if established, makes all other objections fall.

**Template:** "If I can get people to believe that **[YOUR VEHICLE]** is the key to **[THE RESULT THEY DESIRE MOST]** and it's only available through **[YOUR SPECIFIC PRODUCT]**, then all of their objections become irrelevant and they have to give me money."

**Critical data:** Offers focused on ONE thing always outperform. More than one argument = conversion drops 50%+. More than two = nearly zero. (Perry Belcher / Digital Marketer research)

**Crevita's Big Dominos:**
- Playhouse STEM: "If I can get parents to believe that reading AI-themed stories WITH their kids is the key to raising AI-ready children, and the easiest way to start is the Playhouse STEM starter kit..."
- Professional: "If I can get non-technical professionals to believe that a simple AI automation framework is the key to 10x-ing their productivity, and the fastest path is through my consulting..."

## EMAIL MARKETING FRAMEWORKS

### Soap Opera Sequence (First 5 Emails After Opt-In)
A dramatically driven sequence that hooks readers and carries them forward, email to email. Each email ends with a cliffhanger that compels opening the next one. Creates emotional investment before asking for money.

### Seinfeld Daily Emails (Ongoing After Soap Opera Sequence)
Daily emails "about nothing" — named after the TV show because the show wasn't about anything but was entertaining and beloved.

**Structure:**
1. Entertaining story/observation from your day
2. Tie it into your product/service
3. Call to action

> "People want to be entertained. A lot of us marketers think our audience wants fact after fact. That's simply not true." — Brunson

**The Eternal Principle — List Building:**
- $1/month/subscriber rule: 30,000 subscribers = ~$30,000/month
- Email list outlives EVERY platform change (Google slaps, Facebook bans, algorithm shifts)
- Everything you do should drive list building. YouTube, social, courses — all designed to get people on the email list.

**For Crevita:** Mailchimp is built but not deployed. Deploy Soap Opera Sequence for new Gumroad buyers → transition to Seinfeld-style daily/weekly emails sharing AI parenting stories, tips, and product links.

## THREE CORE FUNNELS (Funnel Stacking)

After analyzing 350,000+ funnels, Brunson found 98% of revenue came from three types:

### 1. TRIPWIRE FUNNEL (Low Ticket)
Free + Shipping or $7-$47 offer → Order Form Bump → Upsell 1 → Upsell 2 → Offer Wall
- Goal: Acquire customers at breakeven or profit on day one
- Brunson's stats: $31.10 average revenue per FREE book given away

### 2. WEBINAR FUNNEL (Mid Ticket, $997-$2K)
Registration Page → Thank You Page (sell something) → Indoctrination Emails → Live Webinar → Follow-up Sequence
- $3/registrant via ads, 25% show-up, 10% close rate on live
- Follow-up sequence typically DOUBLES live webinar sales

### 3. HIGH TICKET FUNNEL ($25K+)
Case Study Lead → Application → Phone Call → Enrollment
- Fed by email sequences sharing success stories

**Funnel Stacking:** Tripwire → Webinar → High Ticket. Each funnel is a rung on the value ladder. "$238 earned within six weeks for every free book given away" across all three funnels.

## THE PERFECT WEBINAR (90-Minute Framework)

### Part 1: INTRODUCTION (10 min)
- Start early, high energy, no dead air
- **Bold Promise**: "Before this ends, I'll show you [specific outcome]"
- **Hook to End**: "Everyone who stays gets [gift]"
- Qualify yourself (credentials, social proof)
- **Future Pace**: "Imagine when you have this working..."

### Part 2: CONTENT — Three Secrets (45 min)
Each secret targets a FALSE BELIEF and follows: Identify → Shatter → Rebuild

**Brunson's ClickFunnels example:**
- Secret 1 (vehicle): "How to ethically steal a million dollars worth of funnel hacks" → breaks "funnels are hard"
- Secret 2 (internal): "How to clone a proven funnel in 10 minutes" → breaks "it takes too long"
- Secret 3 (external): "How to get competitors' customers coming to you" → breaks "traffic is hard"

### Part 3: THE CLOSE — The Stack (35 min)
1. Present each component, show its value
2. Stack slide adds each component: "You get [1] + [2] + [3]..."
3. **If-All Statements**: "If all this did was [X], would it be worth $11,552?"
4. Reveal actual price (fraction of stacked value)
5. **Two Choices Close**: Do nothing OR invest
6. **30-Minute Countdown**: 3/4 of all sales come during this Q&A period

**Trial Closes throughout** — embed 1000+ yes/no questions where the answer is obviously YES. "Does that make sense?" "Are you getting this?" Adding trial closes alone increased per-registrant revenue from $9 to $16.

## THE CONTRAST PRINCIPLE (From Charles Atlas)

The power of marketing is in showing the BEFORE and AFTER. The bigger the gap between where someone IS and where they COULD BE, the more desire to buy.

**Pain Amplification:** "Your job is to take somebody who's unhappy and really twist the knife and create more pain" before showing the solution. People won't change unless the pain of staying the same exceeds the pain of changing.

**Apply via:** Before/after images, origin stories showing struggle→transformation, case studies showing the gap.

## FUNNEL MATH & DEBUGGING

When something isn't working, diagnose systematically:

| Metric | Problem Signal | Fix |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| Low click-through on ads | Hook is broken | Test new hooks (images, headlines, pattern interrupts) |
| Low opt-in rate | Landing page offer is weak | Improve the bait, test new lead magnets |
| Low show-up (webinar) | Indoctrination is weak | Add pre-webinar videos, reminders, thank-you page sell |
| Low close rate | Story isn't building belief OR offer is weak | Identify which false beliefs aren't being broken |
| Low upsell take | Upsell doesn't complement the core offer | Ensure logical next step on value ladder |
| High refunds | Over-promised, under-delivered | Fix the product, not the funnel |

**Break-even rule:** "If I don't break even on day one, I don't do the campaign or the funnel."

**Hook fatigue hierarchy** (what wears out fastest to slowest):
1. Creative (refresh every 1.5 weeks)
2. Hook/angle (medium-term fatigue)
3. Offer (slowest to fatigue)

**Dream customer tip:** "Your dream customer is typically you 5 years ago." — Brunson on Young and Profiting

## MIFGE & CONTINUITY MODEL (2025 Evolution)

From Brunson's 2025 Ryan Pineda interview — the Value Ladder has evolved to center on **recurring revenue**:

**MIFGE = Most Incredible Free Gift Ever**
- Used to acquire low-ticket continuity members
- Physical box + digital access for ~$97/month
- "They're getting this huge box of stuff — even more than the $25,000 program"

**Continuity as the Lynchpin:**
- "Regardless of what I do today, every morning I get my Stripe text saying how much we made overnight... $250K whether I do anything or not"
- Dan Kennedy: newsletter is "the tent pole that holds the rest of the tent together"
- "Those who pay, pay attention" — paid subscribers consume more, buy more, ascend more

**Revolving/Tiered Pricing (from Taylor Welch):**
- $10K upfront onboarding + $1,000/month ongoing
- Upfront cash funds experience + ad spend, monthly creates recurring

## AI-ERA UPDATES (2025-2026)

From Brunson's 2025 interviews (Digital Social Hour, Jeremy Miner, Tommy Mello):

1. **AI speeds up funnel building** — What took weeks now takes days. But the FRAMEWORKS haven't changed. Hook Story Offer still works. The Stack still works. AI makes execution faster, not strategy different.
2. **AI-generated copy is detectable** — Audiences are developing "AI radar." Funnels that feel human outperform AI-generated sameness. Use AI for drafts, but inject YOUR voice.
3. **Content-to-funnel is the new meta** — Free long-form content (YouTube courses, mega-depth plays) feeds the top of the funnel better than ads alone. Caleb Ralston generated 36,487 leads from one free 6-hour YouTube course.
4. **Email list is MORE valuable than ever** — Platform algorithms are unreliable. Your email list is the one asset no platform can take from you.
5. **Sales psychology hasn't changed** — People still buy on emotion, justify with logic. The Epiphany Bridge still works. Contrast still works. What's changed is the SPEED at which you can test and iterate.
