# Perfect Webinar Deep Dive

## Webinar Promotion Sequence (Optimal Timing)

**Best days:** Wednesday (live) + Friday (encore/replay)
**Lead time:** 2.5-3.5 days max from first promo to webinar

### JV Partner / Email Sequence:
- **Monday:** Partner emails list → Registration page → Confirmation page (sell something)
- **Tuesday:** Follow-up email
- **Wednesday AM:** Reminder email
- **Wednesday (1hr before):** Reminder + title hack (change webinar title to "**STARTING NOW** [name]" to trigger notification)
- **Wednesday:** LIVE webinar
- **Thursday:** Promote Friday encore
- **Friday:** Encore webinar (replay with live Q&A)
- **Saturday AM:** Replay email
- **Sunday AM:** Replay + cliff notes PDF (screenshots of all slides)
- **Sunday (6hrs before close):** Final urgency — "this offer disappears forever"

**Key stat:** ~1/3 of all sales come in the last 6 hours (urgency/scarcity). Follow-up sequence typically DOUBLES live sales. Example: $80K live → $250K total.

## Registration Page Principles
- **Curiosity is king** — never use video on registration page
- Headline = One Thing + Three Secrets
- Images should NOT make sense (increases curiosity)
- 53% conversion rate achievable on curiosity-based pages
- Re-niche the front end for different audiences (same webinar, different hooks)

## Thank You Page Strategy
1. Video: recap what they'll learn
2. Write down date/time
3. Sell something to cover ad costs (free trial, $27 guide, free book)
4. Goal: get credit card entered BEFORE the webinar (objects in motion stay in motion)

## Indoctrination Videos (3 pre-webinar videos)
1. Show the result (anatomy of a successful funnel/outcome)
2. Show the process (how you do it)
3. Show ease of implementation (do it in 10 minutes)

## Iterative Improvement
After EVERY live webinar:
1. Export all Q&A questions
2. Read EVERY question to find why people aren't buying
3. Tweak slides, add case studies, address objections
4. Do next webinar with improvements
5. Repeat 40-50 times

**Brunson's progression:** First webinar: $30K. After reading Q&A and tweaking (4 hours): $120K same day. Continued: $150K. "If I had done one automated webinar, I would have left millions on the line."

## Performance Benchmarks
- 7-8% close rate = good, makes money
- 10% = million-dollar webinar
- 15% = Brunson's consistent rate (~$10M in <10 months from one webinar)
- 38% = Brunson's best from stage
- Live webinars consistently outperform automated

# Soap Opera Sequence (5 Email Framework)

A dramatically driven email sequence delivered after someone opts in. Each email ends with a cliffhanger.

**Purpose:** Hook new subscribers emotionally and carry them through the first week, building relationship before asking for money.

**Structure:** 5 emails (can extend to 10-15) where each email creates urgency to open the next one. Named after soap operas because they use the same "tune in tomorrow" mechanics.

**Key principle:** The sequence is NOT about teaching. It's about creating emotional investment through storytelling. Each email follows the Hook Story Offer pattern at a micro level (subject line = hook, email body = story, CTA = offer).

# Seinfeld Daily Emails

**When:** After the Soap Opera Sequence ends, transition to daily Seinfeld-style emails indefinitely.

**What:** Short, entertaining emails about everyday observations — tied back to your product/service.

**Why named after Seinfeld:** The show was "about nothing" but was the most-watched show on TV because it was entertaining.

**Template:**
1. Opening story (something from your day — funny, interesting, relatable)
2. Bridge to your business lesson
3. Call to action (link to product, content, or offer)

**Key:** "People want to be entertained. A lot of us marketers think our audience wants fact after fact. That's simply not true." — Brunson

**For Crevita:** Weekly/daily emails sharing:
- Funny moments with your kids discovering AI ("My daughter asked Alexa to do her homework...")
- FAA work stories that illustrate AI concepts (anonymized)
- Italy/Japan cultural observations tied to technology adoption
- Quick tips → linked to Gumroad products

# Value Stack Framework (Detailed)

Instead of creating new products, add ancillary products to increase perceived value.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify your core product
2. Add complementary items that don't cost much individually
3. Address fears/doubts while presenting ("I know you're probably thinking...")
4. Frame each addition with customer-centric benefits
5. Show total value vs. actual price (aim for 10-20% of total perceived value)

**Crevita Example — "Parent's AI Starter Kit" Stack:**
- Core: AI Parenting Guide (ebook) — value $47
- Bonus 1: "How to Put Your Robot to Sleep" children's book PDF — value $15
- Bonus 2: Parent's Activity Checklist (printable) — value $19
- Bonus 3: AI tool recommendation list with tutorials — value $29
- Bonus 4: Private parent community access (30 days) — value $47
- **Total perceived value: $157 → Price: $29 (code LAUNCH)**

# Dream 100 Strategy

Identify the 100 people/platforms/channels where your dream customers already congregate. Then systematically build relationships with those 100 to access their audiences.

**Steps:**
1. List 100 influencers, bloggers, podcasters, group owners in your niche
2. Follow, engage, comment, share their content (warm up)
3. Offer value first (guest content, free tools, testimonials)
4. Eventually get featured on their platform (podcast guest, guest post, collaboration)
5. Their audience → your funnel

**For Crevita / Playhouse STEM:**
- Parenting podcasts (target 20)
- Education YouTubers (target 20)
- AI literacy advocates (target 20)
- Mommy bloggers with email lists (target 20)
- Teacher influencers on Instagram/TikTok (target 20)
