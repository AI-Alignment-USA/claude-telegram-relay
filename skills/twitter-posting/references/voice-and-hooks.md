# Voice & Hook Point Reference

## Crevita Moody's X Voice Profile

### Who She Is (for tone calibration)
- Data Scientist at the FAA, single mom, former Fortune 300
- MBA, lived abroad (Italy, Japan -- 3 years each)
- Author of a #1 Amazon bestseller children's book on AI
- Building Playhouse STEM to make AI accessible to families
- Speaks from experience, not theory

### Voice DNA

**She sounds like:** A sharp friend texting you about something she just read -- equal parts informed and excited, never performative.

**She does NOT sound like:**
- A LinkedIn influencer ("Thrilled to announce...")
- A tech bro ("This is insane. We're so early.")
- A news anchor (flat, neutral, no personality)
- An academic (jargon-heavy, hedged to death)
- A motivational poster ("The future belongs to those who...")

### Tone Markers
- Conversational but credible
- Opinionated but fair
- Curious, not reactive
- Warm but direct
- Occasionally funny, never trying to be funny
- Uses plain English to explain complex things (this is her superpower)

### Sentence-Level Rules
- Contractions always: "I'm, it's, that's, won't, can't, doesn't"
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or "and" instead
- Short sentences punch harder. Use them
- Questions work well as hooks: "Why does no one talk about this?"
- First person when sharing perspective: "I've been thinking about..."
- Okay to start with "So," "Look," "Honestly," "Here's the thing"
- No corporate filler: "leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem," "at the end of the day"
- No AI hype cliches: "game-changer," "revolutionary," "the future is here," "buckle up"

### Perspective Anchors
When posting about AI news, Crevita should come through in one of these modes:

1. **The Translator** -- "Here's what this actually means for regular people"
2. **The Parent** -- "As someone raising a kid in this... here's what I noticed"
3. **The Insider** -- "I work with data and AI every day. Here's what most people miss"
4. **The Advocate** -- "We need to talk about who this helps and who it leaves behind"
5. **The Curious One** -- "I don't have the answer but this question matters"

Not every post needs a perspective anchor. Some stories just need clean delivery with a good hook. But when she has a take, it should feel rooted in one of these modes.

## Hook Point Framework for X

Hook Point is about earning attention in the first 3 seconds. On X, that means the first line of the post must stop the scroll.

### The 7 Hook Structures

**1. The Contrarian**
Challenge conventional wisdom or expected reactions.
- "Everyone's excited about [thing]. I'm worried about [angle]."
- "The biggest AI story this week isn't [obvious thing]."
- "[Company] just did [thing] and nobody's asking the right question."

**2. The Curiosity Gap**
Create an information gap the reader needs to close.
- "There's a detail in this paper that changes everything about how we think about [X]."
- "[Company] quietly shipped something that matters more than [big headline]."
- "Most people will miss why this matters."

**3. The Specificity Hook**
Concrete numbers and details beat vague claims.
- "[Model] just scored [X] on [benchmark]. That's [context for why it matters]."
- "This paper tested [specific thing] across [N] scenarios. The result surprised me."
- "[X]% of parents have no idea their kids are already using AI at school."

**4. The "So What?" Reframe**
Take a news item and immediately tell people why they should care.
- "[Company] released [thing]. Here's why that matters if you're not in tech."
- "New AI regulation just dropped. The part no one's reading is on page [X]."
- "Another model benchmark. But this one actually tells us something useful."

**5. The Pattern Interrupt**
Say something unexpected that breaks the feed's rhythm.
- "I explained [AI concept] to my kindergartner. He understood it faster than most adults."
- "The best AI safety proposal I've read this month came from a [non-obvious source]."
- "Stop calling it artificial intelligence. Start calling it [reframe]."

**6. The Stakes Reveal**
Show what's at risk or what's being decided right now.
- "A decision is being made right now about [X] and most people don't know."
- "This isn't just a product launch. It's a bet on [larger thing]."
- "In 2 years, [prediction grounded in today's news]. Today's [event] is step one."

**7. The Simple Truth**
Sometimes the most powerful hook is radical clarity.
- "[Simple statement about AI]. That's it. That's the post."
- "AI is not coming for your job. It's coming for the parts of your job you never learned to explain."
- "If you can't explain what an AI model does in one sentence, you don't understand it yet."

### Hook Selection by Story Type

| Story Type | Best Hooks | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Product launch | Curiosity Gap, So What? Reframe | Contrarian (unless warranted) |
| Research paper | Specificity, Curiosity Gap | Simple Truth (too reductive for research) |
| Policy/regulation | Stakes Reveal, So What? Reframe | Pattern Interrupt (too playful) |
| Safety/ethics | Contrarian, Stakes Reveal | Specificity (can feel cold) |
| Accessibility | Simple Truth, Pattern Interrupt | Stakes Reveal (can feel alarmist) |

### Hook Testing Checklist

Before finalizing a hook, verify:
- [ ] Would I stop scrolling for this? (Be honest)
- [ ] Does it promise value the post actually delivers?
- [ ] Does it sound like Crevita, not like a "content strategist"?
- [ ] Is it punchy enough for the first 5-7 words to land?
- [ ] Does it avoid clickbait? (Never mislead to get clicks)
- [ ] Would it still work without the link? (The post should have standalone value)

## Thread-Specific Guidelines

### Thread Architecture
- **Post 1 (The Hook):** Stop the scroll. No preamble, no throat-clearing. The hook IS the first post
- **Post 2 (The Context):** Give the background. What happened, what's the news
- **Post 3-4 (The Insight):** This is where Crevita's perspective lives. What most people miss, why this matters, who's affected
- **Final Post (The Landing):** The takeaway + link. End on something memorable or actionable

### Thread Flow Rules
- Each post should make sense if someone screenshots just that one
- Use "1/" numbering only if thread is 4+ posts
- Don't repeat information across posts
- Transitions between posts should feel natural, not formulaic
- If a thread isn't working, it's probably better as a single post

---

## Quote Tweet Guidelines

- Add insight the original post doesn't have
- Don't just agree ("This!" or "So true")
- Don't dunk (even if the original post is wrong -- reframe constructively)
- Keep it tight: the quoted post provides context, so the commentary should be the "and here's what I think" layer
- Good formula: [Your perspective] + [Why it matters]

### Good Single Post (Policy)
"The EU just proposed requiring AI companies to disclose training data sources. If this passes, it changes everything for data contributor rights. Pay attention to this one. [link]"
[Characters: 219/280]

### Good Thread Opening (Safety)
"Everyone's talking about the new model benchmarks. Almost nobody's talking about what happens when these models get deployed in schools without teacher training.

Let me explain why this keeps me up at night. 1/"

### Bad Examples (Do Not Emulate)
- "🚨 BREAKING: OpenAI just released GPT-5! This changes EVERYTHING! 🤯" (hype, all caps, emoji overload)
- "Interesting developments in the AI space today. Here are my thoughts on the latest from Google." (boring, corporate, no hook)
- "Just read a fascinating paper on transformer architectures and their implications for multimodal reasoning in constrained environments." (too academic, no one cares about your reading)
- "AI is going to change the world. Are you ready? #AI #FutureOfWork #Innovation" (LinkedIn energy, hashtag spam)
