## The Two-Column Exercise (Brand Positioning)

**Purpose:** Generate contrarian takes and differentiate from competitors.

**How it works:**
- **Left column:** Things competitors say/do that you disagree with
- **Right column:** Your alternative view

**Example (Caleb's):**
| What Others Say | What I Believe |
|----------------|---------------|
| Chase virality and views | Optimize for trust, not virality |
| Post on every platform daily | Sustainable systems > posting everywhere |
| Fear-based management drives results | Psychological safety > fear-based leadership |
| Niche down to one tiny topic forever | Interest stacking expands your reach over time |
| More followers = more money | Small correct audience > massive disengaged one |

## The Four Rs Framework (Job Descriptions)

When writing job descriptions for content team roles:

1. **Role** — What function do they serve on the team?
2. **Responsibilities** — What tasks do they own daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly?
3. **Requirements** — Skills and experience needed
4. **Results** — Success metrics (responsibilities = inputs, results = outputs)

**Additions:**
- Communication and cadence expectations (e.g., Slack response times)
- Core values alignment

## The Hiring Funnel (6 Steps)

1. **CV + Optional Video** — Video shows personality that CVs can't
2. **Screening Call** — Salary, location, schedule alignment + 2-3 light technical questions
3. **Technical Interview** — Focus on strategic thinking, not just execution
4. **Technical Assessment** — 72-hour deadline, mirrors actual job, same for all candidates
5. **Culture Interview** — Real-life workplace scenarios. "If someone gives direct feedback in front of everyone, how do you respond?"
6. **Final Interview** — With most senior person they'll interact with

**Solo operator shortcut:** Combine steps → screening+technical in one call, culture+final in another, assessment in between = 3-step process.

## 30/60/90 Day Onboarding Plan

### Days 1-30: Orientation
- Company vision, values, workflows
- Core processes, systems, content calendar
- Smaller tasks for quick wins
- Regular check-ins (3x/week)
- Shadow senior team member

### Days 31-60: Taking Ownership
- Execute independently on core responsibilities
- Develop own workflows within framework
- Receive feedback (reinforce good, correct ineffective)

### Days 61-90: Full Integration
- Own the #1 responsibility from job description
- Minimal supervision
- Demonstrate results against KPIs
- 90-day review: celebrate wins, correct issues

**Must-dos:** Provide clear plan before day one. Assign a mentor/buddy. Build a simple onboarding portal (even a Google Drive folder).

## Culture of Ownership (vs. Checklist Army)

> "If you build a checklist army, you're building individuals that only follow instruction and don't take ownership." — Ralston

**Three ways to build ownership:**

1. **Don't solve their problems.** Put it back on them: "If I got hit by a bus, how would you handle this?"
2. **Move from problems to solutions.** Train them to bring "which of these 2-3 solutions do you recommend?" not "how do I solve this?"
3. **Give autonomy with accountability.** Let them fail on non-mission-critical tasks. "They will learn so much more from that than always getting it right based on what you say."

> "When you don't have fear, that is when creativity thrives." — Ralston

## The 10x Leadership Principle

> "Whatever you do, they're going to do 10 times." — Ralston

If you're slow to respond → they're slower. Show up late → they show up really late. Show up in PJs → guess what they'll do.

"If you have a team operating in a way you don't like, take a look at yourself first."

## Teaching as Leadership Development

Three benefits of making team members teach:
1. **Mastery** — If you can't explain it, you don't fully understand it
2. **Learning retention** — You learn more by teaching
3. **Reveals hidden strengths** — Discover who loves sound design, typography, storytelling, etc.

**Implementation:** Weekly training sessions, rotating who leads. Mentorship loops (senior + junior paired). Buy external courses, assign team members to learn and present back.

## Content Assembly Line (5 Steps)

1. **Ideation** — Research + brainstorm
2. **Capture** — Script + film/record
3. **Editing** — Per-platform optimization
4. **Distribution** — Post + engage (authentically, even if only 5 comments/week)
5. **Analyze** — Review performance, note improvements

**Platform Assignment for Teams:**
- Each editor gets primary platform (85% effort) + secondary (15%)
- Best editor → #1 priority platform + #4
- Second best → #2 + #5
- Third → #3 + #6

## The Wrapping Paper Library

Content ideas + proven formats you've saved for inspiration.

**How to build it:**
- Set recurring habit: weekly scroll through oneof10.com and YouTube
- Screenshot ANYTHING that catches your attention (especially outside your niche)
- Store in Notion, Google Docs, or whatever you actually use
- Browse when you need packaging instead of searching from scratch
- Instagram: use Save + Folders for inspiration collection
- Look OUTSIDE your niche — everyone in your niche copies each other

## First Three Videos (For New Creators)

1. **Introduction Video:** 3-5 pivotal career moments, each paired with one actionable lesson. Not a brag reel. Structure: why now → key moments + lessons → what to expect.
2. **Positioning Deep Dive:** Deep breakdown of your core subject through your contrarian belief lens. Minimum 25 minutes. Sets the lens for all future content.
3. **Experimental Video:** Completely different format. Discover what you enjoy AND what audience enjoys.
